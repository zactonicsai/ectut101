Introduction to Shards and Replicas
====================================

An Elasticsearch index is divided into shards. Each shard is a fully
functional Lucene index. This is the unit of parallelism and of scaling:
when you have two shards, half your documents live in one shard and
half in the other. A query runs against every shard and the results
are merged by the coordinating node.

Replicas are copies of shards stored on DIFFERENT nodes. Without
replicas, losing a node means losing data. With one replica per primary,
any single node can disappear and every document is still available on
another node. The cluster will also promote a replica to primary
automatically when a node leaves.

Pick 2 shards as a starting point for small clusters. Pick
replicas = 1 as the absolute minimum for any data you care about.
